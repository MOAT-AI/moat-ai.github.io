# MOAT Homepage Content Design

Date: 2026-04-07
Status: Implemented in first pass, editorial review pending

## Implementation Snapshot

As of 2026-04-11, the homepage files have already moved beyond placeholder copy and now reflect a first-pass public narrative. The implementation remains consistent with this design's core intent:

- confirmed origin in the 2025 Genesis Mission
- seven founding laboratories named in the participants section
- voluntary participation described without inventing onboarding channels
- ecosystem language framed as emerging direction rather than completed product surface

Follow-on work should focus on editorial refinement, public-readiness review, and information architecture decisions rather than re-scoping the initial content strategy.

## Objective

Replace the placeholder homepage copy with a first-pass narrative that introduces the MOAT Collaboration in a way that is credible for DOE and laboratory audiences while remaining welcoming to prospective collaborators from labs, academia, and industry.

## Audience

The homepage should work for two overlapping audiences:

1. DOE, national laboratory, and program-facing readers who need a clear and credible explanation of what MOAT is and why it exists.
2. Potential collaborators from research, software, infrastructure, academia, and industry who need an accessible entry point into the collaboration.

## Content Strategy

The site should present MOAT as an active collaboration with a clear origin and a growing scope, while carefully separating established facts from planned or proposed work.

Editorial stance:

- Use a formal but readable institutional tone.
- Prefer short paragraphs and concrete language over proposal-style density.
- State that MOAT was created in 2025 as part of the US DOE Genesis Mission.
- Describe the collaboration as growing from a seven-laboratory seed effort.
- Frame proposal-related activities as planned or contingent where the source material does so.
- Avoid inventing governance details, participation workflows, or public resources that are not yet verified.

## Section Plan

### Hero (`index.md`)

Purpose:

- Establish MOAT immediately as a real collaboration rather than a scaffold.
- Introduce the collaboration as a cross-institutional effort focused on AI-assisted tools and coordination for particle accelerators.

Planned content:

- A direct title using the MOAT name in full.
- A tagline that balances mission, collaboration, and AI-enabled accelerator work.
- A short note that points readers to the collaboration vision without describing the site as placeholder infrastructure.

### Introduction (`_sections/01-introduction.md`)

Purpose:

- Explain what MOAT is, where it originated, and what it aims to coordinate.

Planned content:

- MOAT origin in the 2025 Genesis Mission.
- Initial seed participation from seven national laboratories.
- The collaboration's role in coordinating AI-related activities for particle accelerators.
- A concise vision statement about connecting institutions, capabilities, and shared priorities.

Boundaries:

- Do not imply finalized organizational structure beyond the source material.
- Do not overstate currently deployed capabilities.

### Participants (`_sections/02-participants.md`)

Purpose:

- Show the current collaboration footprint without overstating membership detail.

Planned content:

- Name the seven laboratories already listed in the source material: LBNL, ANL, BNL, FNAL, JLAB, ORNL, and SLAC.
- Describe the collaboration as growing to include additional participants from national laboratories, academia, and industry.
- Use prose or a restrained table only if it improves clarity without implying unverified roles.

Boundaries:

- Do not list individuals, offices, or responsibilities that have not been verified.
- Do not claim a final roster.

### How To Get Involved (`_sections/03-get-involved.md`)

Purpose:

- Welcome participation while staying honest about the current maturity of public onboarding channels.

Planned content:

- State that participation is voluntary.
- Explain that collaborators may join, contribute, and help shape shared activities.
- Describe likely engagement modes at a high level, such as collaboration discussions, joint technical work, and proposal-aligned efforts.
- Keep explicit calls to action modest until contact paths are confirmed.

Boundaries:

- Do not invent a mailing list, form, calendar, or contribution guide.
- Do not imply a formal membership process unless later verified.

### AI-Enabled Ecosystem (`_sections/04-ai-enabled-ecosystem.md`)

Purpose:

- Describe the emerging technical ecosystem associated with the collaboration.

Planned content:

- Osprey agentic platform.
- Virtual accelerators and digital twins factory.
- Marketplaces and benchmarks for models, skills, workflows, and codes.
- Shared tools with standardized language and interfaces.

Boundaries:

- Frame these as ecosystem elements and direction, not all as completed products.
- Keep the wording ambitious but grounded.

## Voice and Style

- Use "MOAT Collaboration" or "the MOAT collaboration" consistently.
- Prefer "AI-assisted tools for particle accelerators" or "AI-related activities for particle accelerators" over vaguer phrasing.
- Avoid placeholder language once the first-pass draft is written.
- Preserve nuance around funding-dependent proposals with wording such as "if funded," "planned," or "envisioned" when appropriate.

## Risks and Mitigations

Risk: The page could sound too provisional.
Mitigation: Lead with confirmed facts about origin, participants, and purpose before moving into planned ecosystem language.

Risk: The page could overclaim maturity or formal structure.
Mitigation: Separate current collaboration facts from future-facing ecosystem and proposal language.

Risk: The homepage could feel too institutional for new collaborators.
Mitigation: Keep sentences short, explain scope plainly, and use welcoming language in the participation section.

## Verification Plan

- The seven named labs are spelled consistently.
- Current facts and future plans are clearly distinguished.
- No unverified participation channels are presented as live.
- The homepage reads as one continuous narrative rather than four unrelated blocks.
